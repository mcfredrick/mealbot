Monday: Tofu Veggie Stir-fry with Rice

Serves: 3
Active: 20 min Total: 30 min

Ingredients:

  • tofu: 14 oz
  • broccoli florets: 2 cups
  • bell pepper: 1 large, sliced
  • carrots: 2 medium, julienned
  • ginger: 1 tbsp, minced
  • garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • rice vinegar: 2 tbsp
  • sesame oil: 1 tbsp
  • cornstarch: 1 tsp
  • rice: 1.5 cups uncooked

Steps:

  1. Cook rice according to package directions if not pre-cooked.
  2. In a wok or large pan, heat sesame oil over medium-high heat. Add tofu and cook until golden, about 5 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  3. Add ginger and garlic, sauté for 30 seconds. Add carrots and broccoli, stir-fry for 5 minutes.
  4. Add bell peppers and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. In a small bowl, mix rice vinegar, cornstarch, and 2 tbsp water. Add to the pan with tofu.
  6. Stir until sauce thickens and coats vegetables and tofu.
  7. Serve over rice.

Variations:

  • Matt: Base is vegetarian and gluten-free; no changes needed.
  • Lara: Add 1/2 cup cooked shrimp or chicken to the stir-fry.
  • Isla: Finely chop all vegetables and ensure no large pieces. Use no added salt.

Tuesday: Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Serves: 3
Active: 25 min Total: 35 min

Ingredients:

  • sweet potatoes: 2 large, peeled and diced
  • black beans: 1 can (15 oz), rinsed and drained
  • corn tortillas: 9 small
  • avocado: 1 mashed
  • lime: 1 juiced
  • cilantro: 0.25 cup, chopped
  • cumin: 1 tsp
  • chili powder: 0.5 tsp
  • olive oil: 1 tbsp

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Toss sweet potatoes with olive oil, cumin, and chili powder. Roast for 20 minutes until tender.
  2. Warm black beans in a small pot or microwave.
  3. Mash avocado with lime juice and cilantro.
  4. Warm corn tortillas.
  5. Assemble tacos: spread avocado on tortillas, add sweet potatoes and black beans.
  6. Serve immediately.

Variations:

  • Matt: Base is vegetarian and gluten-free; no changes needed.
  • Lara: Add grilled fish or chicken on top.
  • Isla: Ensure sweet potatoes are soft and cut small. Use no added salt.

Wednesday: Gluten-Free Veggie Pasta with Marinara

Serves: 3
Active: 20 min Total: 30 min

Ingredients:

  • gluten-free pasta: 8 oz
  • canned tomatoes: 1 can (14 oz), crushed
  • zucchini: 1 medium, diced
  • mushrooms: 1 cup, sliced
  • basil: 2 tbsp, chopped
  • olive oil: 1 tbsp
  • garlic: 2 cloves, minced

Steps:

  1. Cook pasta according to package directions.
  2. In a pan, heat olive oil, sauté garlic for 30 seconds. Add zucchini and mushrooms, cook for 5 minutes.
  3. Add canned tomatoes and simmer for 10 minutes. Stir in basil.
  4. Toss with cooked pasta.
  5. Serve hot.

Variations:

  • Matt: Ensure pasta is gluten-free; no other changes.
  • Lara: Add pre-cooked meatballs or browned ground meat to the sauce.
  • Isla: Use small pasta shapes and chop vegetables finely. No added salt.

Thursday: Chickpea and Spinach Curry

Serves: 3
Active: 25 min Total: 35 min

Ingredients:

  • chickpeas: 1 can (15 oz), rinsed and drained
  • spinach: 4 cups fresh
  • coconut milk: 1 can (13.5 oz)
  • onion: 1 medium, chopped
  • garlic: 3 cloves, minced
  • ginger: 1 tbsp, minced
  • turmeric: 1 tsp
  • cumin: 1 tsp
  • coriander: 1 tsp
  • rice: 1.5 cups uncooked

Steps:

  1. Cook rice if not pre-cooked.
  2. In a large pan, sauté onion, garlic, and ginger until soft.
  3. Add turmeric, cumin, coriander, and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add chickpeas and coconut milk, simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Stir in spinach and cook until wilted.
  6. Serve over rice.

Variations:

  • Matt: Base is vegetarian and gluten-free; no changes needed.
  • Lara: Add paneer cubes or cooked chicken.
  • Isla: Ensure curry is not too spicy; adjust spices to mild. No added salt.

Friday: Quinoa Salad with Roasted Vegetables

Serves: 3
Active: 30 min Total: 40 min

Ingredients:

  • quinoa: 1 cup uncooked
  • bell peppers: 2 medium, chopped
  • asparagus: 1 bunch, trimmed and cut into pieces
  • red onion: 1 small, sliced
  • lemon: 1 juiced
  • olive oil: 2 tbsp
  • parsley: 0.25 cup, chopped

Steps:

  1. Cook quinoa according to package directions.
  2. Preheat oven to 425°F. Toss bell peppers, asparagus, and red onion with 1 tbsp olive oil. Roast for 20 minutes.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, roasted vegetables, lemon juice, remaining olive oil, and parsley.
  4. Toss well and serve warm or cold.

Variations:

  • Matt: Base is vegetarian and gluten-free; no changes needed.
  • Lara: Add grilled salmon or chicken on top.
  • Isla: Chop all vegetables into small pieces. No added salt.

Saturday: Gluten-Free Veggie Pizza

Serves: 3
Active: 30 min Total: 45 min

Ingredients:

  • gluten-free flour: 2 cups
  • yeast: 1 packet (2 1/4 tsp)
  • water: 0.75 cup warm
  • tomato sauce: 0.5 cup (no added salt)
  • mozzarella: 1 cup shredded (optional)
  • mushrooms: 0.5 cup, sliced
  • bell pepper: 0.5 medium, sliced
  • olive oil: 1 tbsp

Steps:

  1. Preheat oven to 450°F.
  2. Make dough: mix flour, yeast, and water. Knead briefly and let rest for 10 minutes.
  3. Roll out dough on a floured surface to desired thickness.
  4. Spread tomato sauce, add vegetables and cheese if using.
  5. Bake for 15-20 minutes until crust is golden.
  6. Slice and serve.

Variations:

  • Matt: Use vegan cheese or no cheese for low dairy.
  • Lara: Add turkey pepperoni or cooked sausage.
  • Isla: Cut pizza into small squares and use soft toppings. No added salt.

Sunday: Hearty Lentil and Vegetable Stew

Serves: 3
Active: 20 min Total: 30 min

Ingredients:

  • lentils: 1 cup dried or 2 cans cooked
  • carrots: 2 medium, diced
  • potatoes: 2 medium, diced
  • celery: 2 stalks, chopped
  • vegetable broth: 4 cups (no added salt)
  • thyme: 1 tsp dried
  • bay leaves: 2 whole

Steps:

  1. If using dried lentils, cook separately or add to stew with more water.
  2. In a large pot, combine all ingredients except lentils if using canned.
  3. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 20 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  4. Add cooked lentils if using canned, and heat through.
  5. Remove bay leaves and serve.

Variations:

  • Matt: Base is vegetarian and gluten-free; no changes needed.
  • Lara: Add cooked sausage or chicken.
  • Isla: Ensure stew is well-cooked and soft. No added salt.
